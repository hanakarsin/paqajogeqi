Senate Majority Leader Irungu Kang'ata has apologised for the leakage of his warning letter to President Uhuru Kenyatta about the unpopularity of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) in his backyard.

Kang'ata penned the candid letter to the head of state warning him that the BBI would flop in Central Kenya because his research had uncovered the opposition of the document in the region.

On Monday, January 4, the senator confirmed the letter was authentic but explained someone he had copied the letter to leaked it to the press without his consent.

"Kindly though the letter dated 30th December is authentic, I didn't give it to the media. Someone else who had been copied did and I apologise for that," Kang'ata said.

As earlier reported, the Kieleweke allied legislator said the report is unpopular in the region because it is perceived to be Jubilee Party's factional agenda which is only supported by Uhuru's side.

The residents, the senator added, are opposed to the report due to the notion that it aims to expand the executive and increase number of House members.

Kang'ata further noted the anti-BBI team in the area had been speaking the language of the common people in their campaigns while the BBI supporters relied on civil servants who lack political skills to sell their agenda.

His sentiments sparked mixed reactions, especially from the political class.

On his part, Central Organisation of Trade Unions (COTU) secretary-general Francis Atwoli insisted the BBI train will arrive at its destination despite the letter.
