Atheists Support Anglican Church's Move to Ban Politicians from Politicking from Its Pulpits

March 2024 · 2 minute read
  • Archbishop Jackson Ole Sapit barred leaders from speaking at a fellowship event, insisting politicians will no longer misuse the church
  • Atheists said Kenya would only thrive when the secular and religious domains would keep a safe distance from each other
  • Also, the non-believers cautioned Kenyans against Deputy President William Ruto's religious character

Atheists in Kenya Society has backed the Anglican Church's move to ban political utterances from pulpits.

On Sunday, September 12, Anglican Archbishop Jackson Ole Sapit dropped a bombshell after barring ODM leader Raila Odinga and other leaders from speaking at a fellowship event, insisting politicians will no longer misuse the church.

“I have barred the habit of politicians being given the opportunity to speak in any of our churches. I am even advising the members of the media who are busy mounting their equipment, not to bother because they will not talk here. They can address you after the service has ended,” said Sapit.

Weighing in on the matter, atheists said Kenya would only thrive when the secular and religious domains would keep a safe distance from each other.

Warning against Ruto

In a statement, the non-believers cautioned Kenyans against Deputy President William Ruto, whom they accused of cleverly cultivating a Christian nationalistic image, subtly appropriating pentecostal language in his public speeches.

They further accused the second in command of taking advantage of the vulnerability of unemployed youth for his own egotistical political gains.

As a campaign strategy, Ruto has fashioned himself with the low-income earners christened hustlers and largely depends on their votes in his quest to succeed President Uhuru Kenyatta.

Ole Sapit on politicians' donations

TUKO.co.ke also reported when the Anglican Church of Kenya banned politicians from giving their donations to the church.

Ole Sapit, who made the announcement while in Kiambu county said the institution will only accept offerings.

The church head faulted the manner in which politicians had held the church hostage by their huge donations.

“Giving should be a secret thing between you and your God, it’s not something to be displayed," said Ole Sapit. "Do it secretly it does not matter the amount . But if it is for display, we are saying we are sorry because the church is not available for display,” he added.
