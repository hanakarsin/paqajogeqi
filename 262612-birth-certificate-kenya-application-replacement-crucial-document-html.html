Birth certificate application and replacement in Kenya

July 2024 · 6 minute read

We can all relate to some of the uses of the Kenyan birth certificate. Primarily, the document serves as the first form of identification before you finally attain the age of applying for a national identity card. It doesn’t end there. The Kenyan birth certificate number is used when applying for the national ID, passport or visa. So, are you conversant with the process of birth certificate application?

Previously, the application process used to take place at the district registrar of births and deaths office, within the department of civil registration Kenya. This meant long queues for the applicants. Currently, you can apply for the certificate at a Huduma Centre close to you. In the current digital age, applicants can submit their application for this important document online from the comfort of their home or office on the eCitizen portal.

Kenyan birth certificate application requirements

There are several requirements that you need to fulfil when applying for this document. To apply for one, you will be required to present the following:

  • The notification of birth from the hospital or clinic card.
  • The original national ID cards, passports or birth certificates of the parents.
  • If any of the parents are dead, you will be required to present their death certificate.

Individuals over the age of 18 years should present the following:

  • Your ID card
  • Your parents’ original ID, passports, or birth certificates.
  • Form four or class eight leaving certificates.
  • Your baptismal card.
  • A printout from the National Registration Bureau if you can’t access your parents’ IDs.

Application for registration of birth procedure

If you’ve given birth to your baby in any hospital in Kenya, the hospital should offer you a notification of birth. The document is composed of the name of the child, parents’ names, the hospital in which the child was delivered, the date, stamp and an authorized signature from the hospital.

After that, you will need to present the notification of birth to any Huduma Centre in the country to begin the process of Kenyan birth certificate application. Notably, you will need to pay some small fee for the service. This, however, is not the only way you can get your child a birth certificate.

You can also apply for a birth certificate by visiting the region’s district registrar of births and deaths, within the department of civil registration Kenya, which is in charge of all birth records in the country. Here, you will use the exact name as highlighted in the notification of birth or opt to choose a different name, which will cost you a few extra shillings. You will be given a form to fill and attach with copies of the identification documents of the parents as well as the birth notification.

The information you’ll have given will then be verified, and you will be directed to the cash registrar where you’ll make the necessary payments. You’ll pay Ksh. 50 to get a birth certificate without amending the name. If you want to correct the name of the parent, you will pay Ksh. 90, and Ksh. 130 if you're going to adjust both the child’s and the parent’s name. For re-registration, you will pay Ksh. 90.

How to apply for a late birth certificate in Kenya

Late registration of births applies to all those births that are not registered within a period of six months from birth. To apply for a late birth certificate, you will be required to download a late birth registration form and fill it out. The information provided will be reviewed by the registration officials and you will be required to pay Kshs. 150. Once you’re done, you’ll be issued with a receipt that you will be required to present when collecting the birth certificate.

Birth certificate Kenya replacement procedure

What to do if I lost my birth certificate in Kenya? How do I replace a lost birth certificate? These two questions are commonly asked when it comes to matters birth certificate. If you have lost this crucial document, you should visit a Huduma Centre near you. Here, you will be guided on what to replace it and the applicable fees.

Frequently asked questions

The following are some of the questions that people ask during the application of this vital document:

How long does it take to get a birth certificate in Kenya?

You will need to wait for two weeks before the document is ready. However, there are plans underway to ensure that the birth certificates are processed and given to the applicants on the same day that they are formally sought in future.

Can I get a birth certificate at Huduma Centre?

You do not have to visit the civil registration offices to get this crucial document. This service is available in all the Huduma Centre branches across the country. All you need to do is visit the branch that is closest to you, and you will be guided on how to go about the application or replacement procedure.

How can I track my birth certificate application?

It is not possible to track your application if you submitted it at a Huduma centre or registry office. You need to physically visit the offices to check the progress of your application. However, if you submitted the application online, you can track the application online if you log in to your eCitizen account

How do I get my birth certificate without birth notification?

If you do not have a birth notification, there is no need to panic. You should visit the local sub-chief's office where you will be given the necessary documentation. If possible, you should provide any other document that can validate your date of birth such as the baptismal card.

There you have it. A simple and detailed guide on birth certificate application. Follow this guide when applying for a new certificate or replacing a lost one. If you have any issues relating to birth certificate replacement or application, there is no need to panic. All you need to do is to visit a nearby Huduma centre and submit your queries. The registration officials will advise you on the way forward.
