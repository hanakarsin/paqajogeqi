Kenyans online ungovernable following Kiambu Governor Waititu impeachment

August 2024 Â· 2 minute read

- At least 63 MCAs voted to impeach Ferdinand Waititu on corruption allegations

- The governor is facing a court case in which he allegedly presided over loss of KSh 588,198,328

- Kenyan online welcomed the move but expressed fear he may be saved by the Senate

A number of Kenyans online camped on social media platforms to celebrate ouster of Governor Ferdinand Waititu following his surprise impeachment by Kiambu county assembly.

The governor, who is facing a number of economic crimes and other related corruption charges, was voted out office by 63 MCAs on Thursday, December 19.

Shortly thereafter, celebrations that started right on the steps on the county assembly spilled over to the internet where netizens joined in.

A spot check by TUKO.co.ke later on the day, showed the unforgiving online community actually appreciated the ward representatives for kicking out Waititu over corruption.

Their only hope now lies with the Senate where the controversial county boss will have his last word.

The governor's woes started way back in June 2019 when the Director of Public Prosecution Noordin Haji ordered he be arrested and prosecuted.

According to Haji, the governor presided over embezzlement of KSh 588,198,328 in the 2017/2018 financial year.

The case is yet to be determined and ruling made and until then, the governor will remain barred from his office.
