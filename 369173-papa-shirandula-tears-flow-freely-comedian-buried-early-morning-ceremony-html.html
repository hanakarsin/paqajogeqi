Papa Shirandula: Tears flow freely as comedian is buried in early morning ceremony

June 2024 · 3 minute read

- Decorated actor Charles Bukeko succumbed to COVID-19 at a Nairobi hospital on Saturday, July 18

- His body was transported to his Funyula home where the funeral procession arrived at around 3am on Monday, July 20

- Nobody was allowed to view the body which remained in the hearse until 8.30am when MoH officials lowered it in the grave

- Shirandula is survived by his wife Beatrice Andega and three children

The body of popular comedian Charles Bukeko alias Papa Shirandula has been buried at his Funyula home in Busia county.

Shirandula who succumbed to COVID-19 on Saturday, July 18, was interred by Ministry of Health (MoH) officials at around 8.30am.

The funeral procession arrived at Shirandula's home at around 3am on Monday, July 20, and the body remained in the hearse until time of his burial. Not even his family and neighbours were allowed to view the body.

The ceremony took place under the watch of hawkeyed security officers who ensured compliance with coronavirus containment measures.

She also accused the hospital of only conducting a COVID-19 test on Shirandula and ignoring both pneumonia and malaria tests.
