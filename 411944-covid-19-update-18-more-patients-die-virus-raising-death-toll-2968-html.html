COVID-19 Update: 18 More Patients Die from Virus Raising Death Toll to 2,968

February 2024 · 3 minute read

- The Ministry of Health stated that 334 new cases of COVID-19 had been recorded over the past 24 hours

- Some 124 patients had recovered, 85 from the home-based care while 39 are from various health facilities countrywide

- A total of 930,460 people have so far been vaccinated against COVID-19 countrywide

A total of 334 people have tested positive for COVID-19 in the last 24 hours, the Ministry of Health has announced.

Health Cabinet Secretary Mutahi Kagwe said the new cases were detected from a sample size of 4,155. This represented a positivity rate of 8.0%.

In an announcement made on Thursday, May 13, the ministry that out of the 334 cases, 329 were Kenyans while 5 were foreigners.

On a sad note, 18 people succumbed to the disease, bringing the total number of fatalities to 2,968.

"Some 124 patients have recovered, 85 from the home-based and Isolation care while 39 are from various health facilities countrywide. Total recoveries now stand at 113,248, of whom 82,298 are from Home Based Care & Isolation, while 30,950 are from various health facilities," Kagwe stated.

The total number of confirmed positive cases now stands at 164,720, while the cumulative tests done since the pandemic struck is 1,733,500.

In terms of how the virus has been distributed in the counties, Nairobi leads with 81, Meru 39, Kisumu 37, Busia 24, Nakuru 16, Siaya, Mombasa and Kilifi 11 cases each, Kiambu and Homa Bay 10 cases each, Kitui (nine), Uasin Gishu and Makueni (eight cases each), Bomet, Embu and Kajiado (seven cases each) while Nyeri and Kericho had six and five cases respectively.

As of today, Thursday, May 13, a total of 930,460 persons have so far been vaccinated against the COVID-19 disease countrywide; of these, 283,507 are aged 58 years and above, health workers (162,071), teachers (145,796), security officers (78,684) and others (260,403).

In a separate story on Wednesday, TUKO.co.ke had reported that the Ministry of Health announced 15 new cases of the deadly Indian COVID-19 variant in the country.

The infections of the Indian strain were identified from 17 samples tested in the Kenya Medical Research Institute (KEMRI) in Kilifi.

While announcing the development on Wednesday, May 12, the health ministry's acting director-general Patrick Amoth also said genomic sequencing showed the Indian variant did not spread in Kisumu, where it was first detected.
