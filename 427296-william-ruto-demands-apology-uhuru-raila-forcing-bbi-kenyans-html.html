William Ruto Demands Apology from Uhuru, Raila for Forcing BBI on Kenyans

May 2024 Â· 3 minute read
  • Deputy President William Ruto has continued to fault the BBI proponents for forcing the bill to Kenyans
  • Ruto has always held the bill was dangerous , arguing it would hamper democracy by reviving the imperial presidency
  • He asked the document's proponents to apologise to Kenyans for shelving the government's big four agenda and pursuing the flopped bill

Deputy President William Ruto is yet to suspend his tirades on the proponents of the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) after the constitutional amendment bill was quashed by the courts.

He indirectly slammed President Uhuru Kenyatta, ODM leader Raila Odinga and other proponents of the BBI for forcing the bill on Kenyans at the expense of the big four agenda.

Speaking on Thursday at his Karen residence, Ruto called on the president alongside his allies to apologise for shelving the government's initial agenda for Kenyans and instead pursuing BBI.

"We demand an apology from those who pushed BBI on us. We demand an apology from them because they sabotage the big four plan that was supposed to create millions of jobs for ordinary people, create a universal health coverage that would benefit millions of women and children," said Ruto.

"They sabotaged the whole food investment strategy that would benefit many farmers and create opportunities for them to access markets," he added.

Ruto has openly disagreed with his boss on the bill which the latter championed for saying it meant well for the country.

The deputy president has been on record claiming BBI as particularly dangerous to Kenyans and it would have clawed back on the gains that Kenya had made in eliminating an imperial presidency.

He specifically pointed out the proposals in the document such as a head of state appointing lawmakers into the Executive and the creation of the office of the ombudsman, as dangerous to a democracy.

"The BBI was going to roll back all the gains we have made. We fought for almost 30 years against an imperial presidency. The BBI was going to install an imperial presidency in a very unprecedented way," he said last month in Karen during a meeting with Nairobi businesspeople."Let us not lie to ourselves and the people. BBI was dangerous for Kenya and we thank God it failed," he added.

Uhuru on the appellate court verdict

The president faulted the Court of Appeal for denying Kenyans what he said was legitimately in their interest as borne in the BBI bill.

He said the bill's opponents chose to politicise it and mistook it as a tool for a political contest.

Uhuru denied the claims that the document had been fronted to help him prolong his term or propel ODM leader Raila Odinga to the presidency.

The president said he firmly believed in uniting the country and part of that was by having more leadership positions as proposed in the now-moribund BBI document.

"I am sad that for political reasons, we could actually deny our people something that would have improved their livelihoods and made Kenya a more cohesive society," said the president.
