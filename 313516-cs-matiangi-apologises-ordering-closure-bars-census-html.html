2019 Census: CS Matiang'i apologises for ordering closure of bars

- Enumeration will start on the night of August, Saturday 24 and end on August 31

- People will be counted with reference to where they will have spent the night of August 24

- Matiang'i directed social joints be closed from 5pm during the census period

- Kenya is among few African countries which have consistently carried out a census exercise every 10 years

Interior Cabinet Secretary Fred Matiang'i has maintained all social joints shall remain closed after 5pm during the 2019 national census period set to begin on Saturday, August 24.

Addressing media at the Harambee House on Thursday, August 22, Matiang'i, however, apologised to revelers over closure of bars during the said period but asked them to enjoy drinks during the day.

"I am sorry for the inconvenience caused due this exercise. We have to carry out the census. We are asking you by 5pm to proceed home and be counted. Some people are asking me through social media to revise the decision. You can go have your drink between 2pm to 5pm. This exercise only happens once in 10 years," he said.

While acknowledging night prayers in most places, the CS also appealed to Christians to skip such events over the weekend for the time being so that they are enumerated.

"Activities of Sunday and Saturday nights to not just affect those who go to bars. I know some of our fellow Christians who are in keshas (night vigils), please on this particular day, let's shift prayers to our houses so that we can be counted," he added.

Enumeration will start on the night of August, Saturday 24, and end on August 31.

During this period, people will be counted with reference to where they will have spent the night of August 24, which is also known as the reference night.

All persons in Kenya during the reference night including outdoor sleepers, people on transit, individuals in hotels and lodges and in institutions will be included.

However, a child who is born after the mid-night of August 24 will not be counted but a person who dies after the mid-night will be counted as having been present.

Interestingly, a polygamous man will only be enumerated once in the household where he will have spent the August 24 night while his wives will be recorded as head or heads of the households.

Kenya is among few African countries which have consistently carried out a census every 10 years.
