Steve Jobs widow Laurene Powell to give away her KSh 28 trillion before her death

August 2024 · 2 minute read

- Laurene Powell Jobs said her wealth would die with her by the time she breathes her last

- The Apple founder's widow will be giving away Ksh 28 trillion in assets shortly after her death or during her lifetime

- The tycoon said her children were already aware that her wealth would be 'dying' with her

- According to Laurene, she inherited her wealth from her husband who did not care about the accumulation of riches

While most people are working hard to acquire wealth that will help them and their generations, some do not really care about earthly belongings.

It goes without saying that Steve Jobs' widow Laurene Powell is among the richest women in the world.

However, just like her late hubby, she does not care about wealth and has even made plans to distribute it to the less fortunate when she dies.

The Apple founder's widow and one of the richest women in the world said she will be giving away KSh 28 trillion in assets during her lifetime or shortly after her death.

Laurene oversees a sprawling media, political and charitable empire called Emerson Collective.

According to Vox, the rich lady plans to direct Emerson to give away her wealth.

TUKO.co.ke gathered that Laurene said she inherited her wealth from her husband who did not care much about riches.

“I inherited my wealth from my husband who didn’t care about the accumulation of wealth. I am not interested in legacy wealth buildings, and my children know that. If I live long enough, it ends with me.” she said in an interview with New York Times.
