Lawyer Ahmednasir likens BBI push to 1982 coup

Senior Counsel Ahmednasir Abdullahi has likened the push for the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) referendum to the 1982 coup.

The city lawyer said the BBI push and the 1982 attempted coup have similar characteristics and denominators.

On his Twitter page, Ahmednasir said both events were extra-constitutional in that they were not part of the provisions of the Kenyan law.

By being extra-constitutional, the lawyer also meant that just like the 1982 coup by the Kenya Airforce, the BBI report is largely about individuals who want to clinch to power a little bit longer than the law provides.

"BBI and the August 1982 coup attempt have a number of common denominators and characteristics. Both are Extra-constitutional. Give three other shared attributes," said the counsel.

The handshake report was launched on Wednesday, November 27, by President Uhuru Kenyatta and opposition leader Raila Odinga at Bomas of Kenya.

The report, according to the duo, was meant to solve problems that had bediviled Kenya since independence.

However, critics of the BBI pointed out Raila was using the report as a road map to State House in 2022 while Uhuru was using it to remain in power.

The 1982 coup was planned by the Kenya Airforce to overthrow former president Daniel Moi's regime

He ruled Kenya for six hours and was later found guilty of treason and was hanged in 1987.
